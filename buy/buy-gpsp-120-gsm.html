GPSP 120 GSM — Specifications

GPSP 120 GSM combines the corrosion protection of Z120 zinc coating with the enhanced surface quality of skin-pass rolling. The result is a sheet that's smoother, flatter, and better suited for visible or painted applications compared to standard GP 120 GSM.

When to Choose GPSP 120 GSM Over GP 120 GSM

  • Surface will be painted, powder-coated, or laminated
  • Part requires deep drawing or stamping without stretcher strain marks
  • Application demands tight flatness (e.g., façade panels, shelving)
  • Aesthetic appearance matters (no visible spangle pattern)

For standard roofing or ducting where surface finish isn't critical, regular GP 120 GSM is more cost-effective.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much more does GPSP 120 GSM cost than GP 120 GSM?

GPSP typically costs ₹3,000–5,000/MT more than equivalent GP due to the additional skin-pass processing. The exact premium depends on thickness, width, and maker.

Is GPSP 120 GSM suitable for outdoor roofing?

While it can be used, it's over-engineered for basic roofing. The price premium isn't justified unless the roof will be painted or has aesthetic requirements. Standard GP Z120 is recommended for roofing.
