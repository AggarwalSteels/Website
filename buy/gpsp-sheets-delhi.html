What is GPSP (Galvanised Plain Skin Pass)?

GPSP stands for Galvanised Plain Skin Pass. It is a premium variant of GP sheet where the galvanised coil undergoes an additional skin-pass rolling operation after hot-dip zinc coating.

The skin-pass process applies a light cold-reduction (typically 0.5–2%) which:

  • Improves surface smoothness and eliminates yield point elongation
  • Enhances flatness and reduces waviness
  • Provides better paint adhesion and coating uniformity
  • Improves formability for stamping and deep-drawing operations

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between GPSP and GP?

GPSP undergoes an additional skin-pass rolling after galvanising, which improves surface quality, flatness, and formability. It's ideal for applications where the surface will be painted or where the part is deep-drawn.

Is GPSP more expensive than GP?

Yes, GPSP carries a premium of ₹3,000–5,000/MT over equivalent GP sheets due to the additional processing step.
